02 — Phase 1
Build the Commercial Foundation
May – June 2026

Nothing else in this strategy works without Phase 1 being complete. This is infrastructure, not growth. You are building the plumbing before turning on the taps.

Email Nurture Sequence — Live Within 2 Weeks
Priority 1

Right now you are collecting leads with no conversion layer beneath them. Every person who signs up for the Creator Starter Pack and receives nothing of value for 30 days is a dead lead. This is your most urgent fix.

  • Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + deliver the freebie. Warm, human, brief. Tell them exactly what they'll get from being on your list. No selling.
  • Email 2 (Day 2): Pure value. One specific CapCut tip or technique they can use today. Build trust fast.
  • Email 3 (Day 4): Your story. Why Dina & Agnes exist. What you've both learned. Make it personal and real. This is where connection happens.
  • Email 4 (Day 6): Another value hit. Point to your two or three best YouTube videos. Move them deeper into your ecosystem.
  • Email 5 (Day 9): Soft product introduction. Frame the Asset Pack or Sound Effects as a natural extension of what they've been learning. Not a pitch — a recommendation.
  • Email 6 (Day 14): Direct but low-pressure product email. One product, clear benefit, honest CTA. This is your first revenue trigger from the list.
Product CTAs — Built Into Every New Video
Priority 1

Your videos are teaching without selling. That is leaving money on the table. Every video from this point forward must include one contextual product mention — not a hard sell, but a natural "if you want the assets I used here, they're in the description." Specific actions:

  • Sound design videos → link Sound Effects pack in description + mention verbally at natural point in video
  • Colour grading videos → link LUTs pack
  • Motion graphics / text animation videos → link Asset Pack or Motion Graphics
  • General tutorials → mention Creator Starter Pack freebie to drive list sign-ups
  • Every video → pinned comment with either freebie link or relevant product link
Thumbnail System Overhaul
Priority 2

Your thumbnails are consistent but have settled into a formula that limits CTR ceiling. The fix is not a complete redesign — it is introducing two new elements that your current thumbnails are missing:

  • Show the outcome: At least 50% of thumbnails should show the actual edit result — the text animation, the colour grade, the motion graphic. Not just a face with a number. Viewers click when they see something they want to achieve.
  • Stronger facial expressions: Neutral and composed is not clickable. Genuine surprise, excitement, or curiosity reads in a thumbnail at 120px. Review your top 5 competitors' highest-performing thumbnails and study the expression intensity.
  • Develop a visual signature: One recurring design element — a specific frame treatment, colour accent, or layout structure — that makes your thumbnails instantly recognisable as Dina & Agnes in a crowded search grid.
  • Test split packaging: For every new video, create two thumbnail concepts. Publish the stronger one, but keep the data. After 48 hours, if CTR is below 4%, consider swapping to the alternative.
Course Waitlist Page — Live by End of June
Priority 2

You do not need to have built the course to start collecting demand for it. A simple, well-written waitlist page serves three purposes: it validates demand, it builds urgency, and it gives you a warm list of buyers before launch day. Build a single page with:

  • Clear transformation statement: "Go from overwhelmed beginner to confident CapCut editor"
  • Brief curriculum overview — the outcomes, not the modules
  • Waitlist sign-up with a compelling incentive (early bird pricing, bonus lesson, or first access)
  • Mention in every relevant video from July onwards

03 — Phase 2
Accelerate YouTube & List Growth
July – September 2026

With commercial infrastructure in place, Phase 2 is about aggressive but sustainable growth. The goal entering October is a channel at 2,500–4,000 subscribers and an email list of 400–700 warm subscribers. Those numbers give you a viable course launch.

YouTube Content Strategy — The Topic Framework
Core Engine

Not all topics are equal. You need a deliberate mix of three content types working together:

Type 1 — Search Content (40% of output)

Videos targeting specific high-volume CapCut search queries. These are your long-term evergreen assets. They get found months or years after posting. Examples: "how to speed ramp in CapCut", "CapCut colour grading tutorial", "CapCut text animation tutorial". These grow slowly but compound powerfully. Research your top 10 search-driven topics and build them into your schedule permanently.

Type 2 — Browse Content (40% of output)

Videos the algorithm pushes to people who aren't actively searching — compelling hooks, strong packaging, broader appeal. "Your CapCut Edits Look Bad Because of This" is browse content. "5 Text Animations That Look Like After Effects" is browse content. These are your growth drivers. They require stronger thumbnails and titles but have breakout potential.

Type 3 — Authority Content (20% of output)

Longer, more comprehensive content that positions you as the definitive source. "The Complete CapCut Beginner Guide", "Master CapCut in 30 Days". These build deep trust and tend to attract your most committed viewers — the ones who eventually buy. One per month minimum.

The Breakout Strategy
Every month, identify one video concept with genuine breakout potential — a topic with high search volume, weak existing competition, and strong thumbnail appeal. Treat it as your "big bet" for that month. Spend disproportionate time on the packaging. One breakout video can add 500–2,000 subscribers overnight and reset your growth trajectory permanently.
Shorts Strategy — Reintroduction
Discovery Layer

Two Shorts per week. Non-negotiable. Their role is purely discovery and awareness — feeding new eyes into long-form content and the email freebie. Shorts should never replace long-form in your thinking. They supplement it.

  • Source Shorts from proven long-form moments — take the best 45–60 seconds from a video that performed well and repackage it
  • Every Short ends with a verbal or text CTA pointing to the full tutorial or the free pack
  • Keep Shorts under 60 seconds. You have confirmed longer Shorts underperform on your channel.
  • Test one "standalone Short" format per week — a single tight CapCut tip that works without watching anything else. These tend to travel further.
Email List — Scale to 500+ Before Launch
Revenue Engine

At 8–10 sign-ups per week organically, you will have roughly 350–500 subscribers by October without any additional effort. That is the absolute floor. You want to exceed it. Three ways to accelerate:

  • Dedicate one video per month specifically to the freebie — a tutorial that uses the Creator Starter Pack assets, where the natural CTA is "get these free." This single tactic can spike sign-ups significantly in the week of posting.
  • Add the freebie CTA to every video verbally — not just the description. "If you want the free pack I use for this, link is below" mentioned naturally mid-video converts far better than a description link alone.
  • Pre-launch Meta ads in September — a small £300–£500 test campaign driving cold traffic to the freebie landing page in the 4–6 weeks before course launch. This is the one moment where paid traffic makes sense. It can add 200–400 subscribers before launch day.
Course Pre-Production — Start July
Critical Path

The course cannot be an afterthought squeezed into September. Pre-production starts in July while content output continues. This is not optional if October is the deadline.

  • July: Full curriculum map finalised. Every module, lesson, and learning outcome written. No gaps, no vague sections.
  • August: Recording begins. Treat course recording like YouTube videos — same quality standards, same energy. Aim to record 2–3 lessons per week minimum alongside regular content.
  • September: Editing, platform setup, sales page copy, launch sequence writing. This month is production and commercial infrastructure.
  • Platform recommendation: Teachable or Kajabi. Both integrate with Kit (your email platform). Do not build custom. Speed matters more than bespoke.

04 — Phase 3
The Course Launch
October 2026

This is your first major commercial event. Executed properly, it generates £3,000–£8,000 in a single month and establishes the course as a real product in the market. Executed poorly, it generates £400 and demoralises you. The difference is almost entirely in launch preparation, not the course itself.

Pricing — Do Not Get This Wrong
Price the CapCut Creator Mastery Course at £147 minimum. Ideally £197 with an early bird of £147 for the first 72 hours. Anything below £97 signals low value and attracts price-sensitive buyers who are harder to retain and less likely to leave strong reviews. The course covers a comprehensive curriculum that genuinely transforms editing ability. Price it accordingly.
Pre-Launch Sequence — 3 Weeks Before
Launch Infrastructure
  • Week –3: "Course is coming" announcement on YouTube community tab and in email. Build anticipation. Mention the transformation, not the features.
  • Week –3: Dedicated YouTube video about CapCut editing transformation — frame it as the problem the course solves. Ends with waitlist/early access CTA.
  • Week –2: Email sequence begins for waitlist. 3 emails: problem agitation, transformation story, early bird offer reveal.
  • Week –1: Countdown begins. Behind-the-scenes content on Instagram and YouTube community tab. Student success framing. Social proof if you have beta testers.
  • Launch Day: Full email to entire list. YouTube video going live on launch day if possible. Community tab post. All social channels active.
Launch Email Sequence — 7 Emails Over 7 Days
Revenue Driver
  • Day 1: Launch announcement. Transformation focus. Early bird pricing revealed. Hard deadline communicated.
  • Day 2: "Who this is for" email. Be specific. Speak directly to the person who feels lost in CapCut. Show them they are exactly who this course was built for.
  • Day 3: Curriculum deep-dive. Show the breadth. Highlight 3–4 specific lessons that solve burning problems.
  • Day 4: Objection handling. "I'm not sure if I'm ready." "I've tried tutorials before." "Can't I just watch YouTube?" Address these directly and honestly.
  • Day 5: Social proof email. Beta tester testimonials, results, transformation screenshots if available.
  • Day 6: Urgency email. Early bird pricing ends tomorrow. Not fake scarcity — real deadline.
  • Day 7: Final call. Last chance at early bird. Reminder of transformation. Clean, direct, no fluff.
Beta Testing — Run in September
Trust Builder

Recruit 5–10 beta students in September at a heavily discounted rate (£29–£49) in exchange for detailed feedback and a testimonial. This serves three purposes: it validates the course before full launch, it generates social proof you can use in the launch sequence, and it identifies any gaps or confusion in the curriculum before it matters commercially.

Source beta students from your email list and YouTube community. People who are already engaged will be motivated testers.

05 — Phase 4
Compound to £10K Consistent
November 2026 – March 2027

The October launch proves the model. November through March is about converting that proof into consistent monthly revenue by building the evergreen engine that runs whether or not you are actively launching.

Evergreen Course Funnel
Recurring Revenue

After launch, the course does not go dormant. It enters an evergreen funnel — a system where new email subscribers are automatically moved through a sequence that sells the course without you actively launching it. This is the difference between a business and a job.

  • Extend your nurture sequence to include the course offer at Day 21–28 after sign-up
  • Add a course-specific email to every new subscriber who hasn't purchased after 30 days
  • Run a mini relaunch every 6–8 weeks — a 3-email sequence to non-buyers with a 72-hour discount. This alone can generate £500–£1,500 per cycle.
  • YouTube videos from this point should naturally reference the course where relevant — "I teach this in depth inside CapCut Creator Mastery" is a powerful organic CTA when used genuinely.
YouTube — Scale to 5,000–8,000 Subs by March
Growth Engine

By November, if growth has continued at even a moderated rate, you should be approaching or past 3,000 subscribers. The content strategy from Phase 2 continues, with two additional focuses:

  • Double down on what has worked: Review your analytics in November. Which videos have the highest CTR, best retention, and most subscriber conversion? Make more of those. Stop second-guessing data.
  • Collaboration targeting: Identify 3–5 YouTubers in adjacent niches — video editing broadly, content creation, smartphone videography — who have 5,000–50,000 subscribers and approach them for a content swap or collaboration. One well-matched collaboration can add 200–500 subscribers in a week.
  • Produce one "pillar" video per month at 20+ minutes covering a comprehensive topic. These build watch time, subscriber loyalty, and search authority simultaneously.
Product Revenue — Contextual Upsell System
Revenue Layer
  • Every course student should receive a post-purchase email introducing the Asset Pack as a natural next step — they've learned the techniques, now give them the tools. Conversion rate on post-purchase upsell to existing buyers is significantly higher than cold traffic.
  • Bundle the Asset Pack as a course add-on at a discounted rate during checkout. Even 20–30% uptake adds meaningful revenue per launch.
  • Product-specific YouTube videos continue on a rotating basis — one product-adjacent tutorial per month minimum keeps passive sales alive.
Instagram & TikTok — Targeted Role
Support Channels

Do not try to win Instagram and TikTok in the same way you are winning YouTube. That path leads to spread-too-thin syndrome. Their specific roles from November onwards:

  • Instagram: Social proof engine. Course student results, before/after edits, behind-the-scenes. The goal is brand warmth and trust reinforcement for people who find you outside YouTube. DM responses to warm leads are valuable here — a personal reply converts far better than any post.
  • TikTok: Pure discovery. Repurpose your best YouTube Shorts content. Do not create uniquely for TikTok at this stage — the ROI does not justify it until you have more operational capacity.

06 — Risk Register
What Can Break This Plan

A strategy that does not acknowledge its failure modes is not a strategy — it is optimism. These are the real risks, ranked by likelihood and impact.

Risk 1 — Course delayed past October. This is the highest probability risk. Course production is hard to estimate and easy to deprioritise when content demands are high. Mitigation: treat course recording as a non-negotiable calendar block from July, not something fitted around content. If necessary, reduce long-form to one video per week in August and September to protect course production time.
Risk 2 — Email list too small at launch. If you enter October with fewer than 300 warm email subscribers, launch revenue will be significantly lower. Mitigation: start the freebie verbal CTA in every video immediately, and seriously consider the September Meta ads test at £300–£500.
Risk 3 — YouTube growth decelerates sharply. 40% monthly growth will not hold forever. If it drops to 10–15% monthly by Q3, the subscriber and view targets become harder. Mitigation: this is why the email list matters. Your business cannot be entirely dependent on algorithm generosity. Own your audience.
Risk 4 — Course underpriced. Pricing below £97 collapses the revenue maths entirely. At £69, you need 145 sales per month to hit £10k from the course alone. That is unrealistic. Mitigation: commit to £147 minimum now and do not negotiate with yourself about it later.
Risk 5 — Operational burnout. Two long-form videos per week plus two Shorts plus course production plus email plus social is a heavy load for two people. Mitigation: reduce long-form to one per week during course production months. Consistency matters more than volume.
07 — Weekly Operating Rhythm
What the Week Actually Looks Like

Strategy without execution rhythm fails. Here is the sustainable weekly operating structure from Phase 2 onwards:

Content Production Week
Repeatable System
  • Monday: Research and script for Video 1. Thumbnail concept. Review previous week's analytics — what worked, what did not, why.
  • Tuesday: Record and edit Video 1. Film two Short clips simultaneously.
  • Wednesday: Publish Video 1. Write and schedule email broadcast if relevant. Research and script Video 2.
  • Thursday: Record and edit Video 2. Edit and schedule two Shorts.
  • Friday: Publish Short 1. Thumbnail finalise for Video 2. Course production block (July onwards — 90 minutes minimum).
  • Weekend: Publish Video 2 Sunday or Monday depending on your posting schedule. Batch social content if capacity allows.
Operational Note
During August and September, if course production is behind schedule, drop to one long-form video per week without guilt. A delayed course launch costs more than a missed YouTube upload. Protect the launch window.

09 — Final Assessment
Is This Actually Achievable?

Yes. With the caveat that it requires every major component to execute adequately — not perfectly, but adequately. The course launches on time and at the right price. The email list reaches 500+ before launch. YouTube growth continues at a decelerated but still positive rate. Product CTAs go live immediately.

The one scenario where this fails is if the course is delayed to December or beyond. That removes your primary revenue event from the equation and leaves you dependent on digital product sales and a still-growing YouTube channel. That path still gets you to £3,000–£5,000/month by Q1 — which is real and meaningful — but it does not reach £10k without the course.

The other scenario worth naming: a breakout video. One video hitting 100,000–500,000 views between now and October changes everything. It compresses this entire timeline by months. You cannot plan for it, but you can engineer better conditions by consistently targeting topics with breakout potential and packaging them aggressively.

The Single Most Important Action This Week
Get the email nurture sequence written and live. Not next month. This week. Every day it is not running is a day of leads entering a dead end. That sequence, running properly, will generate your first consistent product revenue and build the warm list that makes October's course launch viable. Everything else in this strategy depends on that list being large and warm enough to convert. Start there.

The business is real. The niche has demand. The growth signals are genuine. The gap between where you are and £10k/month is not talent or luck — it is commercial infrastructure that has not been built yet. This document is the blueprint. Execution is everything now.
